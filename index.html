About

Aristides Eliopoulos is Professor of Biology & Genetics and Head of the Department of Biology at the National & Kapodistrian University of Athens (NKUA), School of Medicine, where he leads research at the intersection of genetics, inflammation, and metabolic health. His work is at the forefront of precision medicine, leveraging AI to develop personalized therapeutic and nutritional strategies.

Beyond his prolific academic record—which includes five patents and a distinguished citation count—he has successfully bridged the gap between the lab and the market as co-founder and CSO of the genomics spin-off Genosophy®. He has coordinated multiple high-level National and EC-funded initiatives and remains a cornerstone of Greek medical education through his extensive teaching and textbook authorship.

Current projects include: epigenetic circuits controlling intestinal stem cell fate; hepatic apolipoprotein A-I as suppressor of steatosis and hepatocellular carcinoma; genetics-based nutritional recommendations to modify microbiota signatures.
